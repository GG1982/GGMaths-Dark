I tailor my style of teaching to each student and their goals.

For 11+ students, I use games and rewards to facilitate a comfortable and fun environment. In turn, this allows us to focus on the repetition of exercises to build our speed and strength in problem-solving.

For GCSE students, understanding the core topics, such as algebra, ratios, and numbers, is most important. These serve as our bedrock before we progress to more advanced concepts.

For A-Level students, I teach the entire syllabus as quickly as possible, leaving plenty of time for exam practice. I am proud that no student at this level has ever achieved less than a B, with the majority at A*/A.

About me how i fell in love with tutoring

My love for it began while studying maths at The University of East Anglia. While there, I worked with a charity to help children from underprivileged backgrounds improve in their maths and English.

Watching the growth and progress these children made was incredible and inspired me to pursue this as my job.

I knew if I wanted to make the most impact on a student, it would be in a one-to-one setting. That's because I can change my style to fit the student's needs.

And so, for the last 7 years, I have done that. During this time, I've helped students go from grade 3 to grade 7 in their GCSEs.

I have worked alongside local primary schools, such as Ashmole Primary and Osidge School, teaching small groups of children.

I have helped A-Level students with their STEP and MAT entrance exams to Oxford or Cambridge. And I've helped students at 11+ get into grammar schools.

I'm extraordinarily proud of each of my students and go to the best of my ability to give them the support they're seeking.
